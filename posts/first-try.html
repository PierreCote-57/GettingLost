Our first actual camping trip, we went to Salmon Point Resort, just down the road from our Campbell River home.

Every trip this summer will be a learning experience. From that point of view, it was a success!

  1. Back-in parking, one little drop of water on the backup camera challenged my driving skills with this bigger than a car vehicle
  2. How to you turn on the heat in a van?
    How do you turn off the heat in the van?
    There must be a thermostat somewhere.
  3. Plug in the power, check.
    Plug in the water, oops. Forgot the key.
  4. Bedtime: How do we setup? What about blankets?
    Tomorrow, trip to Canadian Tire for a sleeping bag for me.

The resort is on the water, our site was on the water (Straight of Georgia) and, having backed into it, we had a gorgeous view of the Straight, including the cruise ships that go by in the evening. The marina offers complete services, keeps the boats safe and gives us visitors something nice to look at.

The people at the office/mini-store are friendly, not pushy, yet appropriately generous of advice and explanations. I now kind of almost know how to hook-up the water for next time (the going in and the going out water).
