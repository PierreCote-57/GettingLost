Our journey started years ago with several long/multi-days road trips, staying in hotels along the way. Entering into retirement, we are looking forward to even longer, sinuous trips; the kind where you don’t know where you will be tonight. This is complemented by us living on beautiful Vancouver Island, peppered with camp sites of all sizes. What to do? What to do?

There seems to be only one answer, bring a portable hotel room with us: Works on the highway and works in the woods. That started us on the hunt for a camper/van.

How do you shop for a camper van when you have (let’s call it) little experience in the camping world? Turns out, for the impulsive, that can translate into a relatively straight forward path. Like most things, there is a wide range of possibilities and everyone has some comfort level. The trick is to find your own Goldielock level. That required several days (weeks) of web research, looking at the new and used market, as well as brand, both for the R and the V part RV. This was followed by trips to some local dealers (there are more than you might think, see map). That, of course, meant we had to deal with salespeople, ranging from some attentive and informating all the way down to some annoying.

And so we proceeded to look, identify must have and must not have. Size wize, the decision was simple: Joy must be able to drive it. That limited us to a Van, which in practice meant a Mercedes, Ford or RAM. Each has its own strengths and weaknesses. Our choice settled on the Ford, the quality (and price) middle ground of the three. Then we went on to features, from important to would prefer to undesirable; from trivial to significant. This one is interesting, but the dark wood makes the interior feel too dark. This otherwise nice 8-year-old model is in great shape, but the 4" screen is too small for our aging eyes. The propane thing was a bit of a confusing situation. It seems this is decided by the manufacturer, possibly limiting our choices. While it seems liked for some of its capabilities, the limitations include more challenging to reload than a gas tank, the risk of explosion. We were happy that our final choice did not include a propane tank.

WE settled on a new Coachmen Beyond 22D. And so we are at home, two days from the next road trip to pick-up our van.
