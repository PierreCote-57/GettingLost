Camping

Our journey started years ago with several long/multi-days road trips, staying in hotels along the way. Entering into retirement, we are looking forward to even longer, sineous trips; the kind where you don’t know where you will be tonight. This is complemented by us living on beautiful Vancouver Island , peppered with camp sites of all sizes. What to do? What to do?

There seem to be only one answer, bring a portable hotel room with us: Works on the highway and works in the woods. That started us on the hunt for a camper/van.

How do you shop for a camper van when you have (let’s call it) little experience in the camping world? Turns out, for the impulsive, that can translate into a relatively straight forward path.

Like most things, there is a wide range of possibilities and everyone has some comfort level. The trick is to find your own Goldielock level. That required several days (weeks) of web research, looking at the new and used market, as well as brand, both for the R and the V part RV. This was followed by trips to some local dealers. That of course meant we had to deal with salespeople, ranging from some attentive and informating all the way down to some annoying.

And so we are at home, two days from the next road trip to pick-up our van (pictured above), and now that I write this, wondering what it’s name will be.
