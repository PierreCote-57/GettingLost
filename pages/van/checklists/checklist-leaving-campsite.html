Leaving Checklist

Work through these before you pull out. The goal is a secured interior, everything disconnected and stowed, tanks handled, and a clean site behind you.

Inside

  1. Wash up and stow all loose items — nothing left on counters
  2. Close and latch every cabinet, drawer, the fridge, and the roof vents
  3. Secure electronics, and close all windows
  4. Switch off the water pump and the Truma Combi water heater
  5. Set climate control off (or to its travel setting)

Water & tanks

  1. Disconnect and drain the fresh-water hose, then stow it
  2. If connected, dump the gray (and black, if equipped) tank at the dump station, rinse, and stow the sewer hose
  3. Close all tank valves

Power

  1. Switch off the pedestal breaker, disconnect the shore-power cable, and stow it
  2. Confirm the van is running on house batteries for travel

Outside

  1. Retract the Carefree Eclipse awning (left toggle ON, press Retract) — watch it close and lock fully
  2. Stow chairs, mat, table, and any outdoor gear
  3. Remove the wheel chocks and leveling blocks

Final walk-around

  1. Walk the full perimeter: awning locked, step in, nothing left plugged in or hanging
  2. Check overhead clearance for branches before pulling out
  3. Leave the site clean — pack out all garbage and make sure any fire is cold to the touch
  4. Release the parking brake and go

The details

A few easy things to forget on the way out.

  • Do a roof check — vents closed, nothing stored loose up top
  • Note the campground's checkout time so you are not rushing or late
  • Leave No Trace: the site should look as good as, or better than, you found it
