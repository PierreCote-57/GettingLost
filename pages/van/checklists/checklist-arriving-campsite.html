Arriving at campsite

Work through these in order when you pull into a site. The goal is a level, powered, watered van with the awning out and the campsite scouted before you settle in.

Park & level

  1. Park the van, be mindful of the door, awning and neighbors
  2. Level front-to-back and side-to-side, adding leveling blocks under the wheels as needed
  3. Set the parking brake and chock the wheels
  4. Shut off the engine

Power

  1. If the site has an electrical pedestal, switch its breaker off, connect the shore-power cable, then switch the breaker on
  2. Confirm power is live inside (lights, outlets)
  3. No hookup? You are on the house batteries or generator — remember the Truma Aventa eco AC needs 120 VAC (shore or generator) and will not start on battery alone

Water

  1. If the site has a water tap, connect a potable hose through a pressure regulator to the city-water inlet
  2. Otherwise, confirm the fresh-water tank is filled for your stay
  3. Turn on the water pump and run a tap until it flows clear and steady
  4. For hot water, switch on the Truma Combi (gas, electric, or mix) and give it time to heat

Climate

  1. Confirm the Truma CP plus is set to external control so the Garmin panel can drive it
  2. On the Garmin: Temperature control → Auto → set your target temperature
  3. Open a roof vent or windows for airflow as needed

Outside setup

  1. Extend the Carefree Eclipse awning (left toggle ON, press Extend) — keep an eye on it while it moves
  2. Set out chairs, mat, and table
  3. Scout the site: washrooms, water tap, garbage and recycling, and the dump station

The details

A few things worth checking before you get comfortable.

  • Note your fresh, gray (and black, if equipped) tank levels so nothing surprises you mid-stay
  • On Vancouver Island, store all food and garbage securely — this is bear country
  • Check for a current fire ban before lighting anything, and note the site's quiet hours
