Sproat Lake Provincial Park sits on the north shore of Sproat Lake, 13 km northwest of Port Alberni off Highway 4. It has 65 campsites (59 vehicle-accessible and 5 walk-in) plus the Taylor Arm group site, a boat launch, and two swimming areas in the warm lake water. A trail through a highway underpass links the upper campground to the lower campground and beach. At the lake's eastern end, the K'ak'awin (Hectors) petroglyph panel is one of the finest prehistoric rock carvings in British Columbia.
